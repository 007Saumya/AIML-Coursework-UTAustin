Objective: The objective of this project is to analyse the available dataset in 'CardioGoodFitness.csv', and as an outcome:

  • Come up with a customer profile (characteristics of a customer) of the different products
  • Perform univariate and multivariate analyses
  • Generate a set of insights and recommendations that will help the company in targeting new customers.

  • Similar to the Fitness scores, it is clear that customers with higher usage (score 4 & above) prefer to use the TM798 model, whereas customers with the median usage score (3) prefer to use the TM195 model.
  • The TM195 & TM498 products are used by a wider range of age groups, from 18 to 47, whereas TM798 is used by a smaller range, from 23 to 37.

  • The fitness parameters also have very strong correlations with each other. Recommendations¶

  • The TM195 product is preferred by both men and women equally, with partnered customers being higher in number than single customers. Partnered female customers were higher when compared to males. They are preferred by customers in a wide age range, from 18 to 47, with a median age of 26. The median income of this group is around 47,000, and have an education from 12 to 18 years. They have moderate fitness and usage scores. Therefore, consider marketing TM195 as an entry level product targeted towards the mainstream of relaxed and beginner fitness enthusiasts.

  • From our analyses, we can see only minor differences between the customers who use TM195 and TM498. The median income of TM498 customers is slightly higher than TM195 customers and is around 49,000. Also, TM498 is popular among partnered males mostly when compared to the TM195 product for which partnered females showed a strong preference. While median fitness scores remained same across both customer groups, TM498 users have a higher usage than TM195 users (between 3 & 4). Therefore it is recommended to market TM498 as a mid-level product focused on moderately active customers.

  • However, it is clear from our analyses that TM798 customers are different from the rest of the users. They have higher fitness scores(between 4&5) and usage patterns (between 4&5). They are also highly paid (from 48,000 to 105,0000 and more educated (between 14 & 21 years of education) than the rest of the customer groups. While these users belong to a smaller age group than the other two products (between 23 & 37), their median age is higher than the other two groups. The customer group consists mostly of men than women, and even among men, partnered males prefer the product more than singles. Therefore, this product can be marketed as a premium segment, targeted to advanced fitness enthusiasts.
